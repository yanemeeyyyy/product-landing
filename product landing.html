Pricing

Tenor Trombone

$600

  • Pitch: B♭ (sometimes with F attachment)
  • Size: Medium-sized
  • Bore Size: Medium (0.500"–0.547")
  • Bell Size: Around 8–9 inches
  • Slide/Valves: Uses a slide for pitch changes
  • Sound Bright and versatile
  • Common Usage: Orchestras, jazz, concert bands, marching bands
  • Difficulty: Moderate
Bass Trombone

$900

  • Pitch: B♭ (with F, D, or other attachments)
  • Size: Larger than tenor, wider bore
  • Bore Size: Larger (0.562" or more)
  • Bell Size: Around 9.5–10.5 inches
  • Slide/Valves: Uses a slide, often with extra valves
  • Sound Darker, fuller, more powerful
  • Common Usage: Orchestras, large ensembles, low brass sections
  • Difficulty: More demanding due to weight and air requirements
Valve Trombone

$1200

  • Pitch: B♭ (same as tenor but uses valves)
  • Size: Similar to tenor but with valves
  • Bore Size: Medium (similar to tenor)
  • Bell Size: Similar to tenor trombone
  • Slide/Valves: Uses three piston or rotary valves instead of a slide
  • Sound Similar to tenor but smoother transitions between notes
  • Common Usage: Jazz, brass bands, marching bands, Latin music
  • Difficulty: Easier for those familiar with valved instruments (like trumpet or euphonium)
